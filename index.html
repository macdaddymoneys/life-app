Diorcy Ortiz Life App

A private, personal-use finance dashboard — one household's budget, bills, and spending, gathered in one place.

i. What this is

Software for one household.

The Life App is a self-built personal dashboard used by its author to manage day-to-day finances: monthly budgeting, bill tracking, and spending review. It is not a commercial product — it has no customers, no sign-ups, and no audience beyond the household that built it.

ii. Data access

Read-only, and only what budgeting needs.

To keep the budget accurate without manual entry, the app connects to the household's own financial accounts and gathers account transactions and balances. That is the full extent of its access.

✓
Read-only.

The app can view transactions and balances. It cannot move money, make payments, or change anything at any institution.

✓
Connected through Plaid.

Bank connections are made via Plaid. Bank credentials are entered with Plaid or the institution directly — this application never sees or stores them.

✓
Revocable at any time.

Connections can be reviewed and revoked through Plaid Portal or from the institution's own connected-apps settings.

iii. Privacy

The data stays home.

Financial data retrieved by the app is stored privately on infrastructure controlled by the author and used for exactly one purpose: the household budget.

Data is never sold, shared, rented, or disclosed to any third party. There is no advertising, no analytics resale, and no marketing use of any kind. Data is retained only while it is useful to the budget, and a connected institution can be removed — along with its live connection — at any time.
